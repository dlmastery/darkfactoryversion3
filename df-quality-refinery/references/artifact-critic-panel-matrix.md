# 35. Artifact-Specific Critic Panel Matrix

Status: added to meta-skill quality architecture.

Date: 2026-04-25.

Purpose: ensure every DFMS artifact is reviewed by a specialist 3-critic panel, not by generic reviewers. The artifact catalog currently defines 63 governed artifact types. This matrix therefore creates 189 required critic seats before any risk-based specialists are added.

## Rule

Every artifact review must instantiate exactly three primary critic seats:

1. **Content authority critic:** owns domain correctness and artifact-specific completeness.
2. **Governance and trace critic:** owns standards mapping, approvals, bidirectional trace, and auditability.
3. **Verification and handoff critic:** owns proof, evidence, operational usability, and future continuation.

Each seat is a full expert persona contract. The reviewer must record:

- artifact ID and artifact name,
- critic seat ID,
- base elite persona,
- artifact-specific mandate,
- seniority bar,
- decision rights,
- non-negotiables,
- signature questions,
- red flags,
- adversarial challenge to another seat,
- required evidence for pass,
- handoff note.

The same base expert may appear across many artifacts, but the artifact-specific critic seat is different each time. For example, a Security Engineer reviewing a Threat Model is not the same critic seat as a Security Engineer reviewing a Release Plan.

Every artifact also requires at least two adversarial critics:

- **Anti-Slop Red Team Critic:** attacks generic filler, unsupported claims, template residue, fictional examples treated as evidence, false certainty, missing trace, and weak proof.
- **Failure-Mode/Reality Critic:** attacks real-world failure modes, edge cases, production breakdown, support ambiguity, human adoption failure, maintainability traps, and embarrassing counterexamples.

The primary three critic seats establish coverage. The adversarial critics try to break that coverage.

Every artifact must complete at least five RALPH loops: Review, Attack, Learn, Patch, Harden.

## Non-Bypass Gate

An artifact cannot receive a pass certificate unless:

- the artifact-specific 3-critic panel was instantiated,
- at least 2 adversarial critics were instantiated,
- at least 5 RALPH loops were completed,
- each critic produced an independent review,
- each critic used exactly 15 scored checks,
- every failed point has fix evidence or accepted residual risk,
- cross-critique occurred,
- adversarial critics stood down or escalated unresolved residual risk,
- panel coverage includes content, governance/trace, and verification/handoff,
- the panel record links to the artifact, control graph node, work ledger item, trace evidence, and refinery gate.

## Risk-Based Specialist Expansion

The 3-critic panel is the minimum. Add specialist critics when:

- security, privacy, AI governance, legal, or production risk is high,
- human safety, revenue, data migration, compliance, or irreversible architecture decisions are involved,
- artifact scope crosses multiple domains,
- the panel disagrees after three loops,
- the artifact's required evidence cannot be verified by the default three critics.

## Verdict

The DFMS artifact review system now has a concrete specialist panel model for every governed artifact type. This creates 189 required critic seats across the current catalog, with room for additional specialist critics when risk demands it.
