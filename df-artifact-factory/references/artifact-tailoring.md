# Artifact Tailoring

Always include:
- Requirements or change intent.
- Acceptance criteria.
- Decisions for material choices.
- Traceability.
- Verification evidence.
- Handoff notes.

Add heavier artifacts when:
- The system is regulated.
- The work crosses teams or vendors.
- Production ownership changes.
- The architecture is novel or risky.
- The codebase is brownfield and poorly documented.
- Security, privacy, reliability, money, or safety risk is high.

Combine artifacts when the project is small, but preserve all required information and links.
