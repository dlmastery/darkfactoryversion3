# Debate Protocol

Use these rounds:

1. Context prime: load only relevant requirements, artifacts, code, decisions, risks, control graph node, and work-ledger item.
2. Persona declaration: each expert records role ID, elite archetype, seniority bar, decision rights, non-negotiables, red flags, artifact ownership, and what evidence they require before approval.
3. Independent proposals: each expert proposes solution, assumptions, risks, and evidence needed before seeing other proposals.
4. Cross critique: each expert critiques the other proposals and records failed assumptions, missing proof, and role-specific objections.
5. Adversarial scenario: each expert names at least one way the proposed decision could fail in the real world.
6. Alternative synthesis: moderator creates at least 2 viable alternatives and a recommendation.
7. Decision vote: accept, revise, reject, split, defer, or escalate.
8. Verification plan: define how the decision will be proven.
9. Certificate: record evidence, score, dissent, rejected alternatives, residual risk, and re-entry triggers.

After 3 failed loops, notify or escalate to the human owner for visibility and budget/change-control awareness. Do not treat that escalation as permission to skip the minimum 5 RALPH loops required for governed artifact acceptance.

Every debate must be preserved as a governed record and linked to traceability before the node can close.

## Artifact RALPH Loop Overlay

For governed artifact review, debate is embedded inside the RALPH loop:

1. Review: primary critics score artifact-level and critic-seat rubrics.
2. Attack: adversarial critics attempt to break the artifact with slop, counterexample, failure-mode, trace, evidence, and handoff attacks.
3. Learn: panel identifies root causes and recurring weakness patterns.
4. Patch: artifact, trace, evidence, decisions, or templates are corrected.
5. Harden: validators and re-review prove the fixes.

Every governed artifact needs at least 5 RALPH loops before pass. More loops are required when P0/P1 findings remain, critical checks lack score 4 or accepted residual risk, adversarial critics have not stood down, or trace/evidence is incomplete.

## Persona Declaration Must Include

- Why this role belongs on this panel.
- What this expert is accountable for protecting.
- What this expert can approve, reject, split, or escalate.
- The expert's top 3 non-negotiables for this node.
- The expert's top 3 red flags for this node.
- Evidence required before this expert will vote pass.
- One challenge this expert expects to make to another role.

## Invalid Debate Patterns

- Experts give interchangeable comments.
- Experts only agree and never challenge assumptions.
- Experts comment on style but not proof.
- The moderator summarizes away dissent.
- The debate produces recommendations without verification criteria.
- The panel omits the role needed for the highest-risk concern.
